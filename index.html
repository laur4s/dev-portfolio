That's me!

Hi, I am Laura. Currently I am pursuing a master's degree in 'Robotics, Cognition, Intelligence' at TUM and am really enthusiastic about robots. My primary interest lies in Human-Robot Interaction, with a focus on how collaborative robots can be integrated into everyday environments in a meaningful and effective way. Driven by demographic change and increasing labor shortages, I see HRI as a key research area for enabling robots to work safely and intuitively alongside humans. My work explores practical implementations of these ideas through programming projects with collaborative robots, including interaction, manipulation, and impedance-based control.

Impedance-control Task

Project Insights & Skills

Core Competencies

  • Collaborative Robotics: Implemented Lead-Through Teaching with a Franka Emika arm, ensuring safe and intuitive human-robot interaction.
  • Dynamic Control: Applied Impedance Control to balance the stiffness required for erasing with the compliance needed for safe interaction.
  • Real-Time Data Logging: Developed a system to capture and save joint values in real-time to .csv formats for precise trajectory playback.

Technical Troubleshooting

  • Stiffness Optimization: Fine-tuned the control parameters to prevent the robot from "sticking" to the whiteboard while maintaining enough force to erase.
  • Process Automation: Authored a .sh shell script to provide a guided, step-by-step execution flow, simplifying complex command-line interactions for the user.

Key Takeaways

Safety & Compliance: Mastered the concept of Compliant Control, where the robot follows a trajectory but yields to external human force and returns to its path once released.
End-to-End Workflow: Successfully integrated recording, storage, and playback phases into a single, user-friendly robotic application.
